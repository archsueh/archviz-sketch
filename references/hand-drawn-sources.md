# Hand-Drawn / Sketch Sources (Curated for archviz-sketch)

## Core Hand-Drawn Illustration Libraries (Free, High Process-Trace Potential)

- **Open Doodles** (https://www.opendoodles.com)  
  Free sketchy illustrations, public domain. Excellent base for process-gao / 过程稿. Recolorable, loose line work.

- **Open Peeps** (https://www.openpeeps.com)  
  Hand-drawn character library. Mix-and-match. Perfect for adding imperfect human figures to sketches.

- **Lukasz Adam** (https://lukaszadam.com/illustrations)  
  Free hand-drawn illustrations & graphics. Strong construction-line feel.

- **Stubborn** (https://stubborn.fun)  
  Free hand-drawn character illustrations. Quirky, imperfect lines — ideal for 生草图.

- **DrawKit** (https://www.drawkit.com)  
  Beautiful free vector illustrations & icons. Use for clean line bases then add process traces manually.

## From 400-free-design-resources (Illustrations category)

## Usage in Prompts
Prioritize these as style references in prompt engineering. Always add "visible construction lines, imperfect hand pressure, construction guides, side trials" for process-gao differentiation.

**Attribution Note**: Check each source license before commercial use. Most are free for personal/commercial with attribution or public domain.

## Gotchas (to be expanded from real runs)
- AI models often "clean up" hand-drawn feel — explicitly negative prompt "clean vector, perfect alignment, glossy, no construction lines".
- CJK text in sketches requires specific prompt locking for readability without losing wobble.
